# Lower Back — L4-5 Herniated Disc

## Summary

Herniated disc at L4-5 (probable), likely originating from a stress fracture in the 1990s caused by plyometric training for high jump, long jump, and ski racing. Became significantly worse in 2001, impacting competitive alpine ski racing career.

## Timeline

- **~1990s** — Probable stress fracture from plyometrics (high jump, long jump, ski racing)
- **~2001** — Major worsening, significantly limited ski racing
- **2001–2006** — Continued competitive alpine skiing ([FIS profile](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sectorcode=al&competitorid=34524)) while managing the injury
- **2006** — Failed to qualify for Winter Olympics; retired from competitive ski racing

## Current Status

Chronic but manageable. Years can go by with zero problems. Occasional flare-ups require lying down while working (as a software engineer) for about a week, but these are rare. Minor flare-ups are easily managed with core work, stretching, and gradual return to activity.

Hamstrings are always tight — may be nerve-related (possible sciatic involvement from the disc).

## Management

### What helps
- Staying strong: squats, lunges, RDLs, skiing, wingfoiling
- Core work and stretching (especially during/after flare-ups)
- Gradual build-up after any flare-up

### What to avoid
- Big jumps while skiing
- Hard impacts generally
- Heavy loading without adequate preparation
