# Upper Back — T3/4 Disc Bulge

## Summary

Thoracic spine injury sustained in July 2008 from jumping off an ~18m cliff into the sea. MRI showed a tiny right paracentral disc bulge at T3/4 with minor degenerative changes and Schmorl's nodes at the thoracolumbar junction.

## Timeline

- **Jul 2008** — Injury (cliff jump, ~18m into the sea)
- **~Jul 2008** — Plain x-ray 2 weeks post-injury — normal
- **2008-11-08** — MRI at Charlemont Imaging ([report](../results/2008-11-08-mri-thoracic-spine.md))
- **2008-11-11** — Review with Dr Joe Conway ([notes](../consultations/2008-11-11-conway-upper-back.md))

## Key Findings (2008 MRI)

- Tiny right paracentral disc bulge at T3/4 impinging slightly on right thecal sac (not cord)
- Mild anterior wedging of T10, T12
- Schmorl's nodes at T10, T11, T12 superior endplates
- Degenerative osteophytes at thoracolumbar junction

## Current Status

Chronic but well-managed. The injury never fully resolved — years of pain followed the initial injury. Upper back often cracks and releases tension (when arching back, sometimes when lying on stomach and pushing up with arms). When particularly tight, the spine can feel like it's bulging out. As long as upper back strength is maintained, it's manageable.

## Management

- Keep upper back relatively strong (regular strength training)
- No specific ongoing treatment required
