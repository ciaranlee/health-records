## Indication

Acute thoracic spine injury, four months ago with persistent mid thoracic pain. Outside plain x-ray two weeks after the trauma was normal.

## Technique

T1 and T2 weighted sagittal scans entire thoracic spine and T2 axial scans through the disc spaces from C7/T1 to T10/11 were obtained.

## Findings

- Mild anterior wedging of the bodies of T10 and T12
- Small anterior osteophytes at the margins of T10/T11 and T12
- Tiny Schmorl's nodes at the superior endplates of T10, T11 and T12 vertebral bodies
- Remainder of thoracic vertebrae show normal height, no bone marrow oedema
- Mild signal loss at the T3/4 thoracic disc
- Tiny right paracentral disc bulge at T3/4 level — impinges slightly on the right side of the thecal sac, not on the cord
- Remaining thoracic disc levels are normal
- Normal thoracic cord signal intensity
- Normal bony canal dimensions

## Conclusion

Minor degenerative change at the thoracic T3/4 disc level associated with tiny right paracentral disc bulge. Also degenerative osteophytes and disc changes at the thoracolumbar junction.
